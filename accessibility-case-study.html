Accessibility Research: Financial Inclusion Study

Understanding barriers in fintech products to serve users with disabilities

The Challenge

Financial technology has transformed how people manage money, but these innovations often exclude users with disabilities through design decisions that weren't intentional but were systematic. An estimated 16% of the global population has disabilities, yet fintech applications rarely prioritize their needs.

Key Findings

Finding 1: Screen Reader Compatibility Failures

Banking transfers require manual text entry for account numbers despite recipient history existing in the system. Users with visual impairments cannot access saved recipients through their screen reader.

"I have to ask my partner to read account numbers to me or type them in. It's a 20-minute task that should take 2 minutes. I feel excluded from managing my own finances."
100% of VI users (n=8) could not access recipient list
22 min avg time vs. 2 min for sighted users
37.5% required assistance from sighted person
Impact: Users with visual impairments cannot independently complete transfers

Finding 2: Motor Accessibility Barriers

Users with motor disabilities face multiple challenges: small touch targets requiring precise control, no keyboard navigation, rapid-change content, and time-limited interactions.

Quantified Impacts:

58% struggled with small touch targets
43% failed to tap buttons on first attempt
73% reported high frustration (3.2/10 satisfaction)
4.8x longer average task time

Finding 3: Cognitive Load in Error Handling

Error messages used technical jargon, lacked clear guidance, and created cognitive overload for users with dyslexia or cognitive disabilities.

Example - Current Error Message:

"Account transfer failed. Recipient not found in database. Verify routing number format (XXXXX) matches bank ABA standards."

Recommended Error Message:

"We couldn't find this bank. Try: 1) Search by bank name, 2) Check the account number for typos, 3) Call us for help."

Impact on Completion Rates:

Current: 29% task success rate
Recommended: 87% task success rate

Recommendations & Roadmap

Based on research findings, we recommend a phased approach prioritizing high-impact fixes.

P1 - 6 Weeks

Screen Reader Compliance

  • Add ARIA labels to dropdowns
  • Convert images to accessible text
  • Enable full keyboard navigation
High Impact • Engineering focused
P2 - 8 Weeks

Motor Accessibility

  • Increase touch targets to 48px
  • Add keyboard shortcuts
  • Remove time-limited interactions
High Impact • Design + Engineering
P3 - 4 Weeks

Cognitive Accessibility

  • Rewrite error messages (8th grade reading)
  • Add step-by-step guidance
  • Use consistent terminology
Medium Impact • Content + Design

Implementation Timeline

Key Outcomes

  • Accessibility is not a feature—it's a design principle that benefits all users, not just those with disabilities
  • Systemic barriers can be eliminated with cross-functional commitment and prioritized planning
  • Accessibility improvements benefit 22% of total user base (beyond the 16% with disabilities)
  • WCAG compliance is achievable with structured approach and proper resources
  • Universal design lifts overall product quality and improves satisfaction for all user segments

Limitations & Future Research

Research Limitations

  • Sample size (n=24) limits generalization to broader population
  • Research focused on core user flows; peripheral features not examined
  • Did not test compatibility with legacy assistive technologies
  • Cross-cultural accessibility needs not studied
  • Mobile platform accessibility not included in scope

Future Research Directions

  • Expand research to mobile application platform
  • Conduct longitudinal study tracking sustained behavior changes
  • Test accessibility features with international user cohorts
  • Measure broader business impact on retention and lifetime value
