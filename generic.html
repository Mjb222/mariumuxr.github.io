UX Research, Competitive Analysis & Journey Mapping of Performance Boosting app for Athletes

Project Overview

My Role: User Researcher
Methods: Desk Research
Interview & Journey Mapping Tools: Canva, Otter, pen & paper
Duration: Aug2023-Present

Goal

A start-up called Boosters approached me to learn more about their prospective users, to do competitive analysis, and to design user journey mapping for their performance boosting app for young athletes.

UXR Strategy

By conducting a comprehensive study involving surveys and interviews, I acquired valuable insights into the preferences and concerns of prospective users. This process enabled me to identify significant challenges of young athletes. Moreover, I undertook an in-depth analysis of other competitors in the wellness industry to explore effective strategies to make the app standout and unique from competitors. Subsequently, I design the user journey mapping for the MVP and recommended features that would help the app to provide value proposition unique from competitors out in the market.

Understanding the Product Vision

Booster app’s researchers had conducted a small research with some athletes and had following assumptions about their users:
- Users between 13-23 years old
- Interested in performance increase
Before conducting UXR, I dedicated time to understanding the stresses that young athletes go through in terms of performance, balancing studies, social and peer pressures. As a psychologist with background in sports psychology, I already had deep understanding of wellness challenges that athletes face in terms of performance. Therefore, along with Booster’s app’s team, three main objectives for the research were decided upon:
- Doing Competitive Analysis
- Field Interviews and talk to the users (athletes) to learn more about athletes and their day to day challenges and pressures they go through.
--- Identify opportunities for users by asking them what solutions they are utilizing to boost their performance and increase their overall wellness.
--- How they deal with day to day challenges of balancing sports, study, social circle, family time.

Competitive Landscape: Understanding & Insights

To develop a deeper understanding of the competitive landscape, I conducted a competitor analysis. I compared “Champion’s Mind” and “Mindset” as Flow app indicated these two apps as their main competitors.
During my desk research, I examined that these both apps had good user retention as per rating on the app stores. Thus, i focused on examining their user retention and engagement strategy to understand their value proposition. I also looked at wellness athletes’ communities, feedback on app store, and blogs on the respective competitor apps to understand pain points of users and design opportunities for MVP.

Competitive Analysis

During competitive analysis, I identified following strategies for user engagement, retention, and app optimization:
- Onboarding process explanation for great first time user experience
- Daily encouragement notifications
- Gaming aspect- Streaks
- Awards for achieving a certain task
- Encouragement after tasks completion
- Community engagement
- In-app feedback option

Interview

For understand more in-depth insights I recruited 4 participants for an in-depth interview. Major focus of the interview was on the following points:
- To learn more about athletes and their day to day challenges and pressures they go through.
- Identify opportunities for users by asking them what solutions they are utilizing to boost their performance and increase their overall wellness.
- How they deal with day to day challenges of balancing sports, study, social circle, family time.

User Journery Mapping

After conducting a thorough research and according to my findings, I proposed following design of user journey mapping. Creating user journey maps helped us to visualize the user's interactions with the prospective MVP, highlighting pain points and opportunities for improvement.

Recommendations

I also recommended following value propositions for MVP Personalization:
- Incorporate AI-driven personalization to adapt training plans based on progress and feedback.
- Gamification: Integrate interactive challenges, leaderboards, and rewards to enhance motivation.
- Real-time Feedback: Implement immediate feedback mechanisms during exercises to optimize performance.
- Social Integration: Enable users to connect, share, and compete with friends for a sense of community.
- Clean Interface: Design an intuitive, easy-to-navigate UI that caters to varying user preferences.

Impact

This track of Agile UXR has left a strong legacy, and a comprehensive playbook for the company to leverage to build their MVP on.
